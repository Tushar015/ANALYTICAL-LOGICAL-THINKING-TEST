// about the assessment

Scientifically grounded.
Brutally honest.

The ALT-60 (Analytical & Logical Thinking Test) is a standardised psychological assessment hitting four core dimensions of higher-order cognitive ability. No vibes, just data.

Built on Sternberg's Triarchic Theory and the Cattell-Horn-Carroll (CHC) model, this isn't your average quiz — it's a legit cognitive profiling tool used in academic, clinical, and organisational settings.

It goes hard on measuring how you actually think — not just what you know.

🎓

Academic

Identify cognitive strengths and gaps for targeted learning support.

🏢

Organisational

Assess analytical competency in talent selection and leadership.

🧠

Clinical

Support cognitive evaluation and psychological profiling.

📊

Research

A reliable instrument for studying reasoning and cognitive development.

What we measure.

I
Section I

Problem Solving

Capacity to crack complex problems across numerical, spatial, and lateral domains. Zero guessing, all logic.

  • Mathematical & numerical reasoning
  • Spatial & pattern-based solving
  • Lateral and divergent thinking
  • Constraint-based logic puzzles
II
Section II

Logical Reasoning

Deductive and inductive reasoning. If it doesn't follow logically, you'll catch it instantly.

  • Syllogistic & deductive reasoning
  • Series completion & patterns
  • Analogical reasoning
  • Blood relations & ranking
III
Section III

Critical Thinking

Spot logical fallacies, dissect arguments, and evaluate evidence without getting played by bad takes.

  • Logical fallacy identification
  • Argument strength evaluation
  • Evidence quality assessment
  • Correlation vs. causation
IV
Section IV

Decision Making

Choose wisely under pressure, manage cognitive biases, and make rational calls when it actually matters.

  • Rational choice under uncertainty
  • Cognitive bias recognition
  • Risk & cost-benefit analysis
  • Ethical & contextual judgement
